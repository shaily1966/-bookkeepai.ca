FAQ

Common questions.

What's the difference between AI categorization and deterministic ITC math?

AI categorizes merchant names and transaction types — this involves judgment calls that can occasionally be wrong. ITC calculation is pure math: the province rate × the debit amount. We use Python to compute every ITC deterministically, not AI. Same inputs always produce the same correct output. This is important for CRA audit confidence.

Which banks are supported? What about garbled PDFs?

31 Canadian banks auto-detected including TD, RBC, BMO, CIBC, Scotiabank, Amex, Triangle MC, Tangerine, Simplii, National Bank, and Desjardins. For CIBC and some RBC/BMO statements with CID-encoded fonts that produce garbled copy-paste text, we automatically fall back to Vision API — reading page images directly. Zero manual re-typing required.

Why doesn't it categorize 100% of transactions?

Opaque transaction codes like "AMZN MKTP CA*Z99335U2" or generic "WALMART #3821" don't contain enough information to categorize confidently. Rather than guess wrong — which could trigger a CRA audit — we flag these in the Needs Review tab with specific questions. 85–90% is auto-categorized; the rest gets your accountant's 10 minutes of attention.

Is my client's data safe?

Yes. Personal information is redacted before analysis. Data is encrypted in transit and never stored permanently after your session. For firms requiring complete data isolation, our desktop app processes everything locally on your machine — no data ever leaves your office.

What exactly is in the 10-tab Excel?

Tab 1: All Transactions (formula-linked master sheet). Tab 2: Expense Summary by T2125 line. Tab 3: HST-GST ITC by province. Tab 4: Fixed Assets CCA (purchases ≥$500). Tab 5: Needs Review with specific questions. Tab 6: 4-Way Reconciliation. Tab 7: Statement Validation. Tab 8: T5018 Subcontractor summary. Tab 9: QuickBooks/Xero CSV. Tab 10: Summary overview.

What is 4-way reconciliation?

It matches your credit card statement against your chequing account, receipts, and invoices — identifying matched, unmatched, and duplicate transactions, color-coded green and red. It also provides a reconciliation health score. This is what proves to CRA that every dollar in your records reconciles correctly.

Can accountants use this for multiple clients?

Yes. Many bookkeepers and small firms use BookKeep AI for client statement processing at $9.99/statement. For firms processing 20+ statements per month, contact us about the desktop app — unlimited statements, white-label output, and client data that never leaves your office.

Which provinces are supported for ITC?

All Canadian provinces — Ontario (13% HST), Nova Scotia/NB/NL/PEI (15% HST), Alberta/BC/SK/MB (5% GST), and Quebec (5% GST, QST noted separately). Province is detected per-transaction from the vendor description — a BC vendor on an Ontario card gets 5%, not 13%.
